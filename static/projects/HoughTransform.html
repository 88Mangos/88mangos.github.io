Traditional Algebra Method¶

Traditionally, lines in the x-y plane are represented by y = mx + b. This is the slope intercept form of lines in the x-y plane.
In high school algebra classes, to find a line that passes through two points, you would use the slope formula.

m = (y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1)

Once you solved for m, you could substitute in your values for x, y, and m to find b. Let's do this for our example problem.

m = (1 - 2) / (2 - 3) = -1 / -1 = 1
y = 1x + b
1 = 1 * 2 + b
b = -1

The solution to our problem is y = x - 1.¶

Slope-intercept Parametrization

For the point (3,2):
y = mx + b
2 = 3m + b
b = -3m + 2
For the point (2,1):
y = mx + b
1 = 2m + b
b = -2m + 1

Below are these lines visualized in the slope-intercept parametrized space.

To find the solution to our equation, we find the intersection of the parametrized equations.
b = -2m + 1 and b = -3m + 2
-2m + 1 = -3m + 2
m = 1
b = -2 * 1 + 1 = -1

Thus, our solution is y = x - 1.¶

Rho-theta Parametrization.

ρ = x * cos(θ) + y * sin(θ)
We substitute our (x,y) coordinates in to find our parametrized equations.
ρ = 3 * cos(θ) + 2 * sin(θ)
ρ = 2 * cos(θ) + 1 * sin(θ)
Below are these two equations visualized in rho-theta parametrized space.

To find the solution to our equation, we find the intersection points of our equations, shown on the figure above.
Substituting these back into our original equation of the form ρ = x * cos(θ) + y * sin(θ) is as follows:
Using (-5π/4,-√2/2):
-√2/2 = x * cos(-5π/4) + y * sin(-5π/4) = x * (-√2/2) + y * (√2/2). Dividing the entire equation by -√2/2 gives us
1 = x - y.

Using (-π/4,√2/2):
√2/2 = x * cos(-π/4) + y * sin(-π/4) = x * (√2/2) + y * (-√2/2). Dividing the entire equation by √2/2 gives us
1 = x - y.

Using (3π/4,-√2/2):
-√2/2 = x * cos(3π/4) + y * sin(3π/4) = x * (-√2/2) + y * (√2/2). Dividing the entire equation by -√2/2 gives us
1 = x - y.
After rearranging 1 = x-y,

We find that the solution to our equation is y = x - 1.¶

Let's confirm our solution, y = x - 1, which we have obtained through three different methods.
